Markup Language Basics

Markup languages are the cornerstone of any webpage, online article, or data table. The first markup language was created in 1991 by Sir Tim Berners-Lee. There are many strengths and benefits to using and understanding markup languages and the vocabulary behind them. This webpage was created to educate and inform users on how to identify a markup language, what types of markup languages exist, the benefits of markup languages, and the future of markups on the web.

There are many useful resources online for learning how to use markup languages and build you own websites. These resources are free to the public and can help you better understand the technical aspects of markup languages.
